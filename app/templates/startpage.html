Welcome to the Browsing Behavior Experiment

Thank you for choosing to participate in the experiment. Below are the details of what to expect from it.

This experiment requires you to don the role of an owner of a small manufacturing company who wants to find potential buyers on an online business-to-business platform called “B2BMart”. The platform arranges buyer requests one after the other on your login page as shown below. A buyer request is an instance of a buyer sharing their procurement needs with B2BMart, which it displays to you. If you think that the buyer request is relevant to your business, you may click on the “I am Interested in the Buyer” button on the screen to express your interest in engaging with the buyer.

On your screen, you will find the relevance of each buyer request to your business scored on six dimensions – (i) product relevance, (ii) location relevance, (iii) order quantity, (iv) buyer reliability, (v) potential for future business, and (vi) other products from the buyer. If the total score from all six dimensions exceeds 72, the request is fit for your business, and you may click the “I am Interested in the Buyer” button.

You are asked to play 9 rounds. In each round, your goal is to find buyer requests that are relevant to your business, i.e., find requests where the total score exceeds 72. In order to help you find the requests quickly, the platform attempts to rank requests in descending order of value but may not always succeed in ranking the requests accurately. At any point in the round, you may choose to end the current round and start a new round by clicking the “Start New Session” button at the top of the page.

Clicking this button would display a new list of buyer requests. However, we recommend you to view at least the top 30 buyer requests in each round before clicking the Start New Session button.

You will be provided a fixed compensation of $2 to participate and a variable compensation of $0.20 for every relevant buyer request you find at the end of all 9 rounds. If you click on the select button when the total value is less than 72, there will be a screen freeze for 10 seconds

On the next page, you will see three multiple choice questions to recap the experiment and your task. Please answer all of them accurately to participate.
